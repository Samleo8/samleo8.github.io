Multi-modal Input Systems

At I-Focus we believe in using multi-modal input systems.

There is no one-size-fits-all solution for an efficient, effective and intuitive interface. We need to recognise every input system's advantages and disadvantages, and realise no system is perfect.

Currently, we are only able to use our hands and the few conventional devices such as the mouse, and tablets to control the user interface.

Our project will allow for more direct interaction with our devices. Movements of the body and the eyes can be directly translated into controls for the user interface, which will definitely prove useful, productive and convenient for majority of users.

With I-Focus, the computer is literally just a glance away.

Auto-Scrolling

Auto-Scrolling is a in-site feature that allows you to read on without moving a finger.

You see, the marvel of eye-tracking lies in the way it gives us semi-telepathic powers when controlling an interface.

Eye-tracking makes reading and scrolling very effective and highly intuitive.

By looking at the edges of the screen, we know you want to look on. And with I-Focus' auto-scrolling function, everything is so intuitive you won't even actively think about it.
